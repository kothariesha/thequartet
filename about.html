The Quartet - brain child of Steve Carter, Musical Director, Bass Player, longtime Austin musician. A couple of years ago Steve’s Reggae band, Pressure, did a few reunion shows. During that time Steve told Sebastian Williams...”Man, you ought to be singing Nat King Cole songs. You just have THAT voice” Sebastian was game. Not long after that. Steve’s songwriting partner from the 90’s, Ron Hicks got in touch about playing together again.

Steve: “Initially, Ron and I just got back together to play our old folksy songs but as we would sit there, after doing our repertoire, we started to play old standards…”Do you know this one? Yeah! How about this one?” Then we slowly started incorporating those into our folk sets. Ron Hicks is our musical professor. He went to Berklee College of Music in Boston years ago. Steve picks the songs, Ron tells us how to play’em. His direction is perfect. He and Steve churn along like a rhythm train and grin at each other. It’s…FUN! Donny Silverman just plays great solos. So, Steve called Donny and ran it buy him. His first response was “Steve, you know I don’t really play jazz.” I said “Join the club” (We even considered calling the band “4 Guys Who Have Never Played Jazz”) What it has become is….magical. We have FUN! The pieces fit, beautifully!

For a short time we were joined by Cindy Jon Morse on piano. It was nice having her while we did. She is a wonderful friend and an exceptional musician. When it ended up just being the 4 of us, we found our groove. It’s a full-on learning experience every time we approach songs like: “One For My Baby” and” Under My Skin”- Sinatra, “Do Nothing Till You Hear From Me” - Joe Williams, “My One and Only Love” - John Coltrane and Johnny Hartman, “I Wanna Be Around” - Tony Bennett’ Stars Fell on Alabama”- Louie and Ella and Nat Cole faves “Just You, Just Me” and “I Was a Little Too Lonely”. The band is so much more than we’d hoped for. We are THE QUARTET.

ARTISTS

Sebastian Williams
Sebastian Williams grew up in East Texas where he learned to sing in church, play football and do all the things that Texas kids do when they’re growing up in the Piney Woods. After graduating High School he joined the Air Force, serving two tours overseas. Sebastian is a long-time Austin resident and veteran of the Austin music scene, having worked with The Lotions and been lead singer for the reggae group Pressure. .

Ron Hicks
Ron was born in upstate New York and, at the age of nine, transplanted to Pasadena, Texas during a family move. Ron took up the guitar while living in Austin during the Cosmic Cowboy era. He spent most of his time as an obscure songwriter, eventually penning and co-writing some songs that were recorded by Scandinavian artists. Ron grew to love jazz and swing during an immersive couple of years at music school and has maintained that affection to this day.

Steve Carter
Steve is one of Austin’s best-known and respected musicians. Steve grew up in Baltimore and moved to Austin where his mastery of guitar, bass, vocal stylings and vocal arrangements have been utilized by multiple successful bands including The Lotions and Pressure. Songwriter Freddy Powers took Steve under his wing and for several years Steve was band leader for Freddy’s touring band, Stop the Truck. Steve originated the idea for the quartet and recruited the band members. His role as musical director has been essential for creating its unique sound. In addition to his performances with the quartet, Steve can often be seen performing around town with The Bee Gees tribute band, the Motown Review, Will Taylor and Strings Attached and other Austin great musical groups.

Donny Silverman
Originally a Bostonian, Donny came to Austin in 1982 at the invitation of legendary songwriter, Townes Van Zandt. Following an eclectic muse, his career has included R&B, Reggae, Rock, Punk, Blues, African, Brazilian and improvisational music. Donny brings all these influences with him, and is the seasoning in the musical recipe that is The Quartet.

Mark Eastman
Mark “Dino” Eastwood is a well-known and highly respected sound engineer and bass player. Mark’s reputation for coaxing the best live sound from performing bands is unparalleled in Austin. Mark is the quartet’s secret weapon for making the group sound good, even in the toughest venues.
